In filtration, water is passed through a porous medium, such as sand, gravel, or activated carbon, to remove suspended particles and sediments. These particles can include dirt, debris, and microorganisms that can cause waterborne diseases. Filtration is a highly effective method for removing impurities from water, and it is often used as the first step in the water treatment process. Chemical treatment processes involve the use of chemicals to remove contaminants from water. One of the commonly used chemicals in water treatment is chlorine. Chlorine is a powerful disinfectant that is added to water to kill harmful bacteria and viruses. It is also used to remove odors and improve the taste of water. However, the use of chlorine has come under scrutiny due to its potential health hazards. As a result, alternative chemicals such as ozone, hydrogen peroxide, and ultraviolet (UV) light are being increasingly used for water disinfection. Biological treatment processes involve the use of microorganisms to remove contaminants from water. One of the most commonly used biological treatment methods is the activated sludge process. In this process, microorganisms are added to wastewater to break down organic matter. The resulting sludge is then separated from the water and treated further before being discharged into the environment. Biological treatment is an eco-friendly method and is often used in conjunction with other treatment processes to ensure complete removal of contaminants. Apart from these primary treatment processes, there are also advanced treatment technologies that are used to remove specific contaminants from water. For instance, reverse osmosis is a technology that uses a semi-permeable membrane to remove dissolved salts and minerals from water. This process is commonly used to treat brackish water or seawater and produce clean drinking water. Another advanced treatment technology is ion exchange, which uses resins to remove specific ions from water. In addition to these treatment technologies, various chemicals are used to enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of water treatment. Flocculants, such as polyacrylamide, are then added to clump the destabilized particles together, making them easier to filter out. Other chemicals, such as pH adjusters and corrosion inhibitors, are also used to maintain the desired water quality and prevent damage to the water distribution system.
